International Conference

“Development through Football – Sustaining the potential of the first African World Cup”

Vienna, 23-24 April 2010

Less than two months away from the first FIFA World Cup finals on the African continent, the “Development through Football” conference will draw the attention of the public to the potential contribution of football in addressing poverty and promoting development initiatives.

The 2-day Vienna conference (23-24 April 2010) will bring together football governing bodies, public authorities, coaches and players with NGO experts, researchers and campaigners in the area of Sport-for-Development. The aim is to exchange good practice and to critically assess the role of sport as a resource for achieving the Millennium Development Goals (MDGs).

The participants will come from across Europe and various African countries, including South Africa. The conference is expected to pave the way for a European networking process based on the proactive exchange of knowledge, ideas, experience and expertise

At a time when the football family will be gearing up for the first hosting of the great festival on the African continent, the organisers invite NGOs, grass-root projects, football stakeholders and other interested parties to Vienna to attend the conference.
